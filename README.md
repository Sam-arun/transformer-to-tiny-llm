# Transformers to Tiny LLMs — 30 Day Series

A 30-day learning series: starting from the basics of Transformers and ending with building a small language model from scratch.

Each day's post (as shared on LinkedIn) is saved here as a markdown file under [`posts/`](./posts).

## Roadmap

### Week 1 — Foundations: What is a Transformer?
1. Why Transformers replaced RNNs/LSTMs
2. Tokenization — how text becomes numbers
3. Embeddings — giving tokens meaning
4. Positional encoding — why order matters
5. Self-attention explained simply (Query, Key, Value)
6. Multi-head attention — multiple "perspectives" at once
7. Recap + simple diagram tying steps 1–6 together

### Week 2 — Inside the Architecture
8. Feed-forward networks in Transformers
9. Residual connections & why they matter
10. Layer normalization — stabilizing training
11. Encoder vs Decoder — what's the difference?
12. Masked self-attention (why GPT can't "see the future")
13. Stacking layers — how depth builds understanding
14. Recap: full architecture diagram (encoder + decoder)

### Week 3 — From Theory to Code
15. Setting up your environment (PyTorch/Colab basics)
16. Building a tokenizer from scratch
17. Coding embeddings + positional encoding
18. Implementing scaled dot-product attention
19. Coding multi-head attention
20. Building the feed-forward + residual blocks
21. Assembling your first Transformer encoder layer

### Week 4 — Building Your Tiny LLM
22. Designing a decoder-only architecture (GPT-style)
23. Preparing a small dataset for training
24. Writing the training loop
25. Loss functions & optimization basics
26. Training your tiny LLM (show loss curves/results)
27. Generating text — sampling strategies (greedy, top-k, temperature)
28. Evaluating your model — what worked, what didn't
29. Scaling up — what changes as models grow bigger
30. Series wrap-up — key lessons + what's next (fine-tuning, RAG, etc.)
